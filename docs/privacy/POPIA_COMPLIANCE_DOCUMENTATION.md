# POPIA Compliance Documentation - Kharon Fire and Security Solutions

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the compliance measures implemented by Kharon Fire and Security Solutions with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) of South Africa. POPIA governs the processing of personal information and establishes conditions for lawful processing.

## 1. Legal Framework

### POPIA Requirements
- **Accountability**: Responsible parties must ensure compliance with POPIA
- **Processing Limitations**: Personal information must be processed lawfully and in accordance with conditions
- **Purpose Specification**: Information must be collected for a specific, explicit, and legitimate purpose
- **Further Processing Limitation**: Further processing must be compatible with original purpose
- **Information Quality**: Reasonable steps to ensure accuracy and completeness
- **Openness**: Adequate information to data subjects about processing
- **Security Safeguards**: Appropriate measures to prevent unauthorized access
- **Data Subject Participation**: Right to request access to and correction of personal information

### 4.1 Technical Measures
- **Encryption**: All personal data encrypted in transit and at rest

- **Secure Development**: Secure coding practices and regular security testing
- **Vulnerability Management**: Regular patching and security updates

### 4.2 Organizational Measures
- **Staff Training**: Regular POPIA and data protection training
- **Access Policies**: Need-to-know basis access restrictions
- **Incident Response**: Established breach notification procedures
- **Background Checks**: Security clearance for personnel with access
- **Confidentiality Agreements**: Binding confidentiality obligations

### 4.3 Physical Measures
- **Secure Facilities**: Controlled access to premises
- **Document Storage**: Locked cabinets and restricted access
- **Disposal Procedures**: Secure destruction of physical documents
